When to Visit Emergency?

Recognizing the signs of a medical emergency can save lives. Seek immediate help if you observe:

Heart Attack Signs

  • Squeezing chest pain or pressure
  • Pain radiating to jaw, neck, or left arm
  • Shortness of breath & cold sweats
  • Nausea or lightheadedness

Breathing Issues

  • Severe asthma unresponsive to inhalers
  • Choking or gasping for air
  • Bluish lips or face (Cyanosis)
  • Inability to speak in full sentences

Stroke (F.A.S.T)

  • Face drooping on one side
  • Arm weakness or numbness
  • Speech difficulty (slurred)
  • Sudden severe headache & confusion

Severe Trauma

  • Heavy, uncontrollable bleeding
  • Deep wounds or gunshot/knife wounds
  • Compound fractures (bone visible)
  • Head injury with loss of consciousness

Acute Abdominal

  • Sudden, intense abdominal pain
  • Vomiting blood (coffee ground color)
  • Bloody or black, tarry stools
  • Rigid or tender stomach area

High Fever / Sepsis

  • Fever > 103°F (39.4°C) in adults
  • Any fever in infants under 3 months
  • Fever with stiff neck & headache
  • Severe shivering or confusion

Severe Allergy

  • Swelling of tongue, lips, or throat
  • Difficulty swallowing or breathing
  • Widespread hives or rash
  • Fainting or dizziness (Anaphylaxis)

Poisoning & Bites

  • Snake bites or unknown insect bites
  • Drug overdose (prescription/recreational)
  • Swallowing household chemicals
  • Sudden seizures or convulsions
